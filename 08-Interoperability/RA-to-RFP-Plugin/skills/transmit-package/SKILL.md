# Transmit Package

## What this produces

The cover that routes a finished tender package to the buyer (and on to the funder): a **transmittal memorandum** (1–3 pages) and a **short cover email**. The memo is also the **decision record** — it is where the procurement-vehicle choice and the open decisions are documented so the choice is defensible and nothing is lost.

## The transmittal memo — structure

1. **Header block** — To / Cc (the authority's head and procurement committee; the funder's task team) / From / Date / Subject / classification (Draft for review).
2. **Purpose** — transmitting the draft package for review and approval, for completion in the funder's standard document and submission for no-objection.
3. **Documents transmitted** — a short table of the files, noting any that **supersede** an earlier draft (so the old one is withdrawn).
4. **Recommended procurement vehicle** — the recommendation (e.g. supply/IS RFP with rated criteria) with a two-to-three-sentence rationale, **and the option considered and not recommended** (e.g. consulting QCBS — obligation of means) with its one-line reason. This is the decision trail.
5. **Design choices already settled** — the things the buyer doesn't need to re-litigate: scope boundary, single turnkey + advisory/capacity as deliverables, independent IV&V retained separately, self-contained requirements, result-obligation machinery, local-capability approach, funding-window schedule.
6. **Decisions for the authority to close before issuance** — the open `[confirm]` items: Data Sheet values, annex/legal-text finalisation, appointing the IV&V agent, pilot-agency commitments, hosting, post-funding sustainability, and completing the funder's bidding-document wrapper for no-objection.
7. **Time sensitivity** — the funding-window/closing-date reality and the critical-path message (launch and pre-clear with the funder now).
8. **Requested action** — a short numbered list (review and confirm the vehicle; close the open decisions or authorise defaults; we complete the wrapper for no-objection), with a sign-off block.

## The cover email — keep it short

Route the memo; don't repeat it. One short paragraph on the recommendation, one on the timing, the attachment list, and an offer to walk the committee through it. Use bracketed placeholders for recipients/names. Note that attachments are added manually (a drafting tool can't attach the files).

## Doctrine to preserve

- **Document the decision; don't ship the reject.** Record that the alternative vehicle was considered and why it was rejected — do **not** attach a stale, contradictory alternative document. Offer a one-page options comparison only if the committee formally wants it.
- **Make the open decisions explicit.** The buyer should see exactly what they must confirm; never bury policy choices.
- **Flag superseded files.** Tell the buyer which earlier draft to withdraw, to avoid version confusion.
- **Lead with the recommendation, not the history.** The memo is for a decision, not a narrative.
