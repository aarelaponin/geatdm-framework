# Local Participation Designer

## The tension to resolve

Governments rightly want local and smaller firms to compete and to build national capability. But a national platform carrying an **obligation of result** still needs real delivery capability, and most funders run **open international competition** where you cannot mandate local content or apply domestic preference beyond narrow, rule-bound limits. The job is to **enable local participation through team structure and scored criteria, while protecting delivery** — not to lower the bar in a way that risks the platform or breaches the rules.

## The four design moves

1. **Encourage JVs and subcontracting; qualify on combined capacity.** Experience and financial thresholds may be met by the **combined** capacity of the JV members (and named subcontractors), not by any single firm. This lets a local-led team qualify by partnering. State the exception in move 2 alongside it so the rule reads cleanly.

2. **Protect the critical capability.** The one capability the platform cannot be delivered without — the platform-delivery / mediator (X-Road/GovStack) experience — **must sit with a jointly-and-severally-liable JV member**, not be met by a subcontractor alone. A specialist may *supplement* it only as a **named, committed, non-replaceable** team member alongside a substantial prime. This is the line that stops a small prime holding the bond while the only real capability sits with a subcontractor the buyer has no recourse against. A local firm may still lead — provided the critical capability sits with a liable member.

3. **Score skills transfer on substance, not nationality.** Re-anchor the inclusion criterion on the **knowledge-transfer outcome** — measurable training, embedding of local/buyer staff in delivery, and sustainment — that *any* bidder earns by genuinely investing in local capability. This ties to the engagement's real capacity objective and is defensible to the funder, whereas a criterion keyed on nationality is not.

4. **Keep qualification proportionate.** Set financial thresholds (turnover, liquidity) to the realistic contract value and cash flow, not inflated multiples; assess at team level. Gold-plated thresholds exclude capable local teams for no delivery benefit.

## What to produce

- The **qualification clauses** (JV/subcontracting, combined capacity, the prime-capability rule with its carve-out) for the RFP's supplier-qualifications section.
- The **scored criterion** wording ("skills transfer & local capability development", on substance) and its weight for the rated-criteria evaluation.
- The matching **Bid Data Sheet** entries — and a one-line consistency note so the general combined-capacity rule and the prime-capability exception don't read as a contradiction ("...except the critical platform-delivery capability — see [ref]").

## Doctrine to preserve

- **Enable, don't mandate.** Within open competition, local participation is encouraged and scored, not required; no domestic preference beyond the funder's rules.
- **Never detach capability from accountability.** The critical capability sits with a liable prime/JV member; a subcontractor is a supplement, not the holder.
- **Reward the outcome, not the passport.** Score knowledge transfer and local-capability investment, earnable by any bidder.
- **Proportionate, not gold-plated.** Thresholds protect delivery; they do not exclude capable local teams gratuitously.
- **Watch the wording.** A general rule plus an exception must be stated so a literal reviewer can't read a contradiction.
