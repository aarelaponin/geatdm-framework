# TK-DPI-01: DPI Ecosystem Map Template

The DPI Ecosystem Map is the principal output of Method Step 1. It documents what currently exists, what is in pipeline, and how the elements relate.

```
COUNTRY DPI ECOSYSTEM MAP
[Country name]; [Date]

1. NATIONAL DIGITAL STRATEGY
   1.1 Strategy document reference (title, version, date)
   1.2 Strategic objectives
   1.3 Cross-references to sectoral strategies

2. EXISTING DPI INITIATIVES (per pillar)
   For each pillar (Access, Data, Interoperability, Identity, Governance):
   2.x.1 Initiatives operational
   2.x.2 Initiatives in pipeline (committed funding)
   2.x.3 Initiatives proposed (no committed funding)

3. INSTITUTIONAL LANDSCAPE
   3.1 Central digital authority
   3.2 Sectoral ministry involvement
   3.3 Independent regulators (data protection, audit)
   3.4 Operating authorities for foundational platforms (national ID, payments)

4. INTERNATIONAL COMMITMENTS
   4.1 50-in-5 country commitments
   4.2 AU DTS implementation
   4.3 Donor-funded programmes (World Bank, EU, USAID, GIZ, FCDO, UNICEF, etc.)

5. STAKEHOLDER MAP (per T59 tier model)
   5.1 Tier 1 — Champions
   5.2 Tier 2 — Early Adopters
   5.3 Tier 3 — Observers

6. EARLIER ASSESSMENTS
   6.1 World Bank assessments
   6.2 ITU assessments
   6.3 OECD assessments
   6.4 EU assessments (where applicable)
   6.5 Internal government assessments

7. DATA-COLLECTION PLAN FOR STEP 2
   7.1 Per-pillar evidence sources identified
   7.2 Interview targets identified
   7.3 Workshop schedule
   7.4 Document-review schedule
```

# TK-DPI-03: Stakeholder Engagement Plan (DPI tier mapping)

Adapt T59 to the DPI context. The DPI programme's tier mapping:

| Tier | Stakeholders | Engagement |
|------|--------------|------------|
| **Tier 1 — Champions** | Ministry of ICT / Digital Authority (programme home); Ministry of Finance; Cabinet Office; supervising minister; the largest sectoral SDA from each pillar's domain | Weekly working sessions; bi-weekly Steering Committee; quarterly Cabinet briefing |
| **Tier 2 — Early Adopters** | Sectoral ministries (Health, Education, Tax, Justice, Customs); Data Protection Authority; National Audit Office; National Statistics Office | Bi-weekly working sessions; per-pillar workshops; sector workshops |
| **Tier 3 — Observers** | Other ministries; civil society; private-sector technology associations; banking association; international development partners (UNDP, ITU, World Bank, GIZ, EU); academia | Monthly bulletin; quarterly community-of-practice |

The full engagement template follows T59 §5 with these tier substitutions.

# TK-DPI-06: Gap Analysis Report Structure

```
GAP ANALYSIS REPORT
[Country name]; [date]

1. SUMMARY OF CURRENT STATE (cite Assessment report)

2. TARGET STATE
   2.1 Per-pillar target maturity level (with rationale)
   2.2 Roadmap horizon (5–10 years)

3. PILLAR-BY-PILLAR GAP STATEMENTS
   For each pillar: from current Level X to target Level Y; sub-components
   where gap exists; evidence base for the gap.

4. PAERA PRINCIPLES MAP
   Cross-check the gaps against PAERA §5.2 ten principles. Identify
   gaps that relate to specific principles (Once-Only, Whole-of-Government,
   etc.).

5. DEPENDENCIES BETWEEN GAPS
   Inter-pillar and intra-pillar dependencies.

6. PRIORITY MATRIX (cross-reference TK-DPI-07)

7. RECOMMENDATIONS
   7.1 Top-3 Wave-1 gaps to close
   7.2 Top-3 Wave-2 gaps to close
   7.3 Wave-3+ gap closures
```

# TK-DPI-11: Beacon Project Template

A beacon project is a concrete, visible, multi-pillar deliverable that demonstrates DPI in operation and builds political support. Standard pattern:

```
BEACON PROJECT: [name]
Wave: ___
Sponsor: ___
Implementation lead: ___

OUTCOME
   Citizen / business / government outcome stated in measurable terms

PILLARS TOUCHED
   Identity: __________ contribution to outcome
   Data: __________
   Interoperability: __________
   Access: __________
   Governance: __________

USE CASE
   Specific government service or process improved

SUCCESS METRICS (90-day, 12-month, 24-month)
   Specific quantitative targets

INVESTMENT REQUIRED
   CapEx ___ + OpEx ___

DEPENDENCIES
   Wave 1 foundational items required for the beacon

VISIBILITY PLAN
   Communication strategy
   Citizen-facing announcements
   International peer-presentation opportunity
```

Common beacon-project patterns: enhanced digital business registration (touches Identity, Data, Interoperability, Governance); learner registry rollout (Identity, Data, Interoperability, Governance, Education sector); cross-government tax-payment status check (Identity, Data, Interoperability, Tax sector); maternal-health appointment service (Identity, Access, Data, Health sector).

# TK-DPI-12: Validation Workshop Agenda

Adapted from TK-35. Standard 1-2-day workshop. Day 1 morning: assessment findings presentation. Day 1 afternoon: Wave 1 deep-dive. Day 2 morning: Wave 2 onwards plus investment plan. Day 2 afternoon: feedback consolidation and key decisions.

Participants: Tier 1 ministries; Tier 2 representatives; donor partners; civil society and private-sector observers as appropriate.

Outputs: feedback consolidation document; agreed amendments to the Roadmap; Council adoption-ready package.

# TK-DPI-14: DPI Summit Deck Template

Public-facing summary of the roadmap. 30–50 slides, structured as:

```
1. Country DPI vision (3 slides)
2. Current-state summary (5 slides — one per pillar)
3. Target-state vision (5 slides — one per pillar)
4. Wave-based roadmap (8 slides — one per Wave plus summary)
5. Beacon projects (3–5 slides)
6. Investment plan summary (3 slides)
7. Governance structure (2 slides)
8. International alignment (50-in-5, AU DTS, etc.) (3 slides)
9. Call to action (citizen, business, donor) (2 slides)
10. Q&A and reference slides (rest)
```

The DPI Summit deck is the principal communication artefact for the roadmap. It is presented at the country's DPI Summit (typically held annually) and used for international peer engagement at events like the GovStack International Forum, the AU Digital Transformation Forum, and the 50-in-5 country-collaborative events.
